What is an industry, anyway?

Deep Read · Finshots

The Supreme Court just reopened a 48-year-old question that affects every hospital worker, teacher, and government employee in India

Back in 1978, a group of water board employees in Bangalore asked a simple question: are we "workmen"? That question just landed before a nine-judge Supreme Court bench. Well, let's find out why the answer still isn't settled.

The Triple Test that created a docket explosion

The 1978 Bangalore Water Supply v. R. Rajappa judgment created what came to be known as the Triple Test. To determine whether something is an "industry", ask three questions: Was the activity systematic and continuous? Did it involve employer-employee cooperation? Did it produce goods or services that satisfy human needs?

"If Parliament had already moved on with the new Industrial Relations Code, what exactly was the Court being asked to settle?" — Finshots

The consequences were vast. Hospitals became industries. Universities became industries. Even temples. That single 1978 ruling gave millions of workers in the public sector the right to raise disputes and seek protection from arbitrary dismissal. But it also opened the door for unions to call strikes in essential services — water, healthcare, schools.

Flexi-Cap Funds: Are They Really Large-Cap Biased?

Flexi-cap mutual funds are required by SEBI to invest at least 65% in large-cap stocks. But most fund managers invest significantly more than that — because large-caps offer stability and liquidity during downturns. Investors who want more mid- or small-cap exposure typically use multi-cap or dedicated small-cap funds. Flexi-cap is deliberately positioned as a "stability with some growth" category.
