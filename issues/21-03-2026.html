No trading today — here is the week in numbers

Saturday Edition · 21 March 2026

Indian equity markets are closed today. The week ended with Nifty at 23,114 (+0.49%) on Friday after one of the more dramatic weeks of 2026. The index crashed 3.26% on Thursday as HDFC Bank's chairman abruptly resigned citing "happenings and practices" he couldn't reconcile with his values — wiping out over ₹65,000 crore in market cap in a single session. The rupee printed a fresh all-time low of ₹93.49/dollar. The week's one piece of structural good news: India's bond yield curve did exactly what it was supposed to do, pricing in a "higher for longer" rate environment.

No Aftermarket Report or Groww Digest on weekends. Today's Brief covers ETAuto's full Saturday roundup, The Ken's deep AI column, Finshots' weekly wrap, and MakeUseOf's Saturday tech reads.

Rare earth magnets, luxury EVs, hydrogen, and Ola's IPO fund diversion

10 stories
Lead · ETAuto · Saturday

The government released the incentive structure for its ₹7,280 crore rare earth magnet RFP — and China just made the urgency clearer

You see, India currently imports 100% of its rare earth magnets — the components inside every EV motor, wind turbine, and piece of defence equipment. Not only has the government now opened bids for the ₹7,280 crore RFP, it has also published the incentive structure: OEMs must establish the full value chain from NdPr oxide to finished magnets. In addition to this, a government pilot plant is already operational at ARCI in Hyderabad. Due to this timing, one more piece of context matters: China's rare earth magnet exports spiked sharply ahead of the Trump-Xi diplomatic meeting — using supply as leverage just as India is trying to build independence from it.

The week's best reads — AI layoffs, LPG markets, and bananas

6 stories
The Ken · Zero Shot · by Praveen Gopal Krishnan · Today

Oracle, Amazon, and Meta are laying off thousands. In the same breath, they're announcing record AI infrastructure spending. Humans are the budget.

You see, there is a new template for the modern tech layoff. Oracle is cutting thousands citing a "cash crunch" from AI data centre expansion. Meta is reportedly weighing a 20% workforce reduction to fund AI infrastructure. Amazon laid off 16,000 employees the same month its CEO stated that AWS revenue is expected to grow at twice the rate previously projected. Not only this — Amazon's Andy Jassy made the logic explicit in an internal town hall: capex for data centres must be laid out years before monetisation. Put simply, humans are being liquidated to fund the infrastructure that AI will eventually run. The Ken's Praveen Gopal Krishnan calls it clearly: the ingredients for social backlash are getting real now. Read the full column on The Ken.
